Zooki

Zooki

Group Project for Database Systems Course at William & Mary, 2015

Responsibilities:
  • Designed database using an entity-relationship diagram
  • Created database and populated tables with data for individual players as well as tournament and regular season games between each NCAA school
  • Constructed algorithms using three point percentage, field goal percentage, free throw percentage, steals, rebounds, combined offense, and combined defense
Conclusions:
  • Used statistics through 2013 to predict the outcome of 2014 tournament games and compared our predictions to actual results
  • Most accurate algorithm was based on three point percentage, correctly predicting 38 out of 67 games
  • From our data, we concluded that it is difficult to predict more than half of the game outcomes using a single variable, and even harder when offensive and defensive variables are combined

Languages/ Technology: SQL, Linux commands

Zooki

Group Project for Game Design course at William & Mary, 2014

Summary: Zooki the penguin's favorite food is ice cream, but it is melting because of global warming. In order to survive, he needs to store as much ice cream as possible where it will be safe and cold in his igloo. Run, jump, and slide to navigate the maze-like terrain and collect enough ice cream cones before they melt. But watch out - the floor is lava!

Responsibilities:
  • Character and background story development; named Zooki
  • Created sprite sheets, graphics, concept art, and sound effects
  • Helped with level designs
  • Wrote design document, made slides for project presentation

Languages/ Technology: C++, Photoshop, Visual Studio
